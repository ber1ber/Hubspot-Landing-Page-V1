## ICP Tests (Two Active Campaigns Running in Parallel)

### ICP 1: HubSpot Users
- **Campaign name:** "Your CRM Has No Voice"
- **Target:** SMB HubSpot users, 10–50 employees (primary), 50–200 (secondary)
- **Persona:** Sales Leaders — Head of Sales, Sales Manager, Founder-led sales
- **Core pain:** Speed-to-lead failures, missed follow-up, manual task execution, underperforming HubSpot workflows
- **Core message:** HubSpot captures leads but most teams don't call them fast enough. SalesAi plugs into HubSpot workflows and turns lead data into real conversations automatically.
- **Offer:** Free AI Workflow Build for HubSpot (build first inbound lead AI calling workflow inside HubSpot for free)
- **Budget:** $12k/month — $10k LinkedIn, $2k Reddit/Google
- **90-day targets:** 50–75 demo bookings, 20–40 opportunities, ≤$300–720 cost per demo, ≤$2k CAC
- **Success metric:** Demo bookings + pipeline (not closed deals — longer buying cycle)
- **Channels:** LinkedIn Ads, Reddit/Google, HubSpot community, HubSpot User Groups, partner distribution (agencies, RevOps consultants), executive LinkedIn (2x/week)
- **Assets needed:** Landing page, 4 HubSpot Automation Blueprints, Supademo workflow demos, LinkedIn/Reddit ad creative, webinar
- **Campaign start:** ~3/9 (2 weeks after Home Services)
- **Landing page structure:** Hero ("Your CRM Has No Voice") → Problem (Why HubSpot Leads Go Cold) → Solution (How SalesAi Works in HubSpot) → Blueprints/Demo → Offer (Free Workflow Build) → CTA (Book Your HubSpot Demo)
- **Blueprint series:** Inbound Lead Response, Pipeline Reactivation, Missed Call Recovery, AI Qualification
- **Fail signal:** <40 demos by Day 60, <25% demo→opp conversion, >$800 cost per demo

### ICP 2: Home Services
- **Target:** Roofers, plumbers, HVAC, and similar field service businesses
- **Core pain:** Miss calls while on the job, lose revenue after hours, can't always pick up
- **Core message:** SalesAi answers instantly, qualifies, books, and hands off — fits the way you actually work
- **90-day target:** 15 closed-won deals (shorter cycle, transactional buyer)

## Revenue Funnel Project Plan (CEO Doc — April 2026)

Prepared for Sterling Curran. This is the master plan for fixing the funnel.

### Current State (The Problem)
- **724 landing page views, 1 conversion = 0.14% CVR**
- LP vs. Facebook form test: LP did not perform
- Core issues:
  - LPs not aligned with ad messaging or campaign intent — HubSpot-hosted page worst performer
  - Same generic form used across all campaigns — no segmentation, no ICP alignment
  - No inbound nurture system — only cold outbound sequences exist
  - Follow-up emails are transactional, robotic, leads don't remember who SalesAi is
  - Ad-to-funnel disconnect: 8-second ads → immediate generic form → context lost
  - No campaign-specific funnels — everything shared across markets and channels

### Funnel Architecture (3-Phase System)
**Phase 1 — Day 0 (Immediate opt-in):**
- AI Voice Call fires instantly — lead experiences the product firsthand
- Welcome email — short video (<2 min), who SalesAi is, single CTA, sent from a real rep's name

**Phase 2 — Weeks 1–3 (Sales-led):**
- 5-email sequence, automated but personal-feeling, one CTA per email, sent from named rep
- Full copy written for both SaaS and Home Services (in the project doc)

**Phase 3 — Post Week 3 (Marketing-led):**
- 1–2 emails/month, softer cadence, case studies/product updates/industry insights, occasional soft demo CTA

### Landing Page Strategy
- **Target CVR: 3–5%** (from current 0.14%)
- Separate LP per campaign AND channel (e.g., /hubspot-linkedin, /hubspot-reddit, /home-services-facebook)
- Headline must mirror the exact ad copy verbatim or near-verbatim
- Above-fold structure: outcome headline → one-sentence sub-headline → 30–60 sec product video → single CTA button
- CTA language: "Get a Free Demo" or "See It in Action" — NOT "Submit" or "Contact Us"
- Form: 3–4 fields max, embedded inline, campaign-specific, micro-copy under CTA ("No credit card required")
- Remove nav bar from all landing pages — only action = CTA
- Mobile-first (60%+ of Reddit/Facebook traffic is mobile)
- Social proof: 2–3 customer logos + one testimonial quote + one metric result

### A/B Tests to Run
- Headline: Outcome-focused ("Close 3x more deals") vs. Pain-focused ("Stop losing leads to slow follow-up")
- CTA: "Get a Free Demo" vs. "See It in Action"
- Form length: 3 fields vs. 4 fields (+ qualifying question)
- Video vs. static image
- Logos only vs. logos + testimonial quote

### Channel Strategy Summary
| Channel | Primary Market | Launch | Notes |
|---------|---------------|--------|-------|
| LinkedIn | B2B SaaS | Week 2 | Tue–Thu, 60% SaaS / 40% Home Svc |
| Reddit | B2B SaaS | Week 2 | Conversational tone, text-heavy ads |

| Google Ads | Both | Week 3–4 | High-intent search, 50/50 split initially |

### Key Metrics & Targets
| Metric | Current | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Landing page CVR | 0.14% | 3–5% |
| Email open rate | Unknown | 35–45% |
| Email click rate | Unknown | 5–8% |
| Speed to first touch | >1 hour | <2 min (AI call) |
| Demo booking rate | Not tracked | 15–20% of leads |

### Key Principles (from CEO)
- Speed wins: hit every lead within 60 seconds of opt-in
- Message match matters: ad → LP → form → email must feel like one continuous conversation
- Campaign-specific everything: no shared LPs, forms, or sequences across markets or channels
- Start simple, then segment: get the machine running first
- First 3 weeks = sales-led, post week 3 = marketing-led
- Track every handoff: ad → LP → form → email → meeting
